Theseus: Hero of the Labyrinth

Theseus is known for two main reasons:

  1. Being the demigod son of Poseidon
  2. Slaying the Minotaur while imprisoned in the Labyrinth

Technically, Theseus had three parents. His mother is the mortal woman Aethra, and he has two fathers,
a mortal father Aegeus, who was king of Athens, and a divine father, Poseidon, god of the seas.

He rose to glory when he volunteered to be an Athenian sacrafice to the Minotaur,
whom he killed in the center of the Labyrinth. This myth is also where English gets the word clue, as the way that
Theseus found his way out of the Labyrinth was by laying out a line of string that he could follow back to the exit,
and in Greek, the word for a spool of string is "clew".
